Pets
 Pets are an integral part of both the American society and economy; more than half of households in the United States have pets. During times of emergencies or disasters, people have been known to risk their lives and the lives of others to save their pets. The best thing you can do for yourself and your pets is to be prepared.

Each type of disaster requires different measures to keep your pets safe. Depending on the type of emergency you may need to briefly leave your home or permanently evacuate. DO NOT LEAVE YOUR PETS BEHIND! Left on their own most pets are not likely to survive, and those that might survive are not likely to be located after a disaster by their owners when they return.

For these reasons it is critical that individuals to actions to properly prepare themselves and their animals for emergency or disasters to save both human and animal lives.

All pet owners are urged to keep a pet emergency supply kit which should include the following:
· Three or more day supply of food in airtight waterproof containers and drinking water.
· Bowls for food and water.
· Current photo and physical description of your pets, including details on markings.
· Medication, vaccination records (especially rabies records) and first aid pet supplies.
· Comfort items such as a toy and blanket.
· Small garbage bags.
· Keep a list of emergency phone numbers (veterinarian, local animals animal shelters.
· For dogs include a leash, harness, and a sturdy carrier large enough to use as a sleeping area.
· For cats include a litter box and litter, as well as a sturdy carrier large enough for transport.
Do not wait until the last moment to evacuate, as rescue officials may not allow you to take your pets with you during a rescue. Make sure to securely fasten a current identification tag to your pet’s collar. It is also a good idea to include a phone number of a friend or family member that has not had to evacuate, so they can be contacted if pet is lost and whoever your pet can not be reached. If you and your pets cannot stay together arrange for friends, family members, veterinarians, or boarding kennels in a safe area to provide foster care.
